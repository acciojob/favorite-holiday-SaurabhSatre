Pune, Maharashtra


Pune, known as the "Oxford of the East" and "Cultural Capital of Maharashtra," is a vibrant city that blends rich history with modern development. Nestled in the Sahyadri mountains, Pune offers a perfect mix of educational institutions, historical landmarks, and natural beauty.

Top 5 Attractions:

  1. Shaniwar Wada - The historic fortification built in 1732, known for its architectural grandeur.
  2. Sinhagad Fort - A picturesque hill fortress with breathtaking views of the surrounding valleys.
  3. Aga Khan Palace - A monument of national importance where Mahatma Gandhi was imprisoned.
  4. Dagdusheth Halwai Ganpati Temple - The iconic temple dedicated to Lord Ganesha.
  5. Lavasa - A planned hill city near Pune with beautiful lakeside views.

Must-Try Foods:

  • Misal Pav - Pune's signature spicy curry served with bread.
  • Bakarwadi - Crispy spicy-sweet snack from Chitale Bandhu.
  • Pithla Bhakri - Traditional Maharashtrian chickpea flour curry with millet bread.
  • Puran Poli - Sweet flatbread stuffed with lentil and jaggery filling.
  • Vada Pav - The legendary Mumbai-style burger that's equally popular in Pune.
